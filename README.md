# Quantum Genesis Theory

This repository contains the first structured draft of *Quantum Genesis Theory*, a conceptual framework that explores reality as an intermediate and non-static manifestation of quanta.

The central idea is that observable matter may not be permanently stable in itself, but may instead result from continuous transitions of quanta between observable and non-observable states.

To illustrate the underlying intuition, consider a blacksmith striking a piece of red-hot iron on an anvil—an image that evokes maximum solidity. 
Yet, at the atomic level, both the hammer and the iron are composed of structures that are overwhelmingly empty: an atom is more than 99.999999999999% empty space, with mass concentrated in a tiny nucleus and electrons occupying diffuse regions. 
What we perceive as contact between solid objects is, in reality, the interaction of these outer electron regions, not the collision of compact matter. 
In this sense, the apparent solidity of everyday reality is not a consequence of fullness, but the emergent effect of underlying structures that are, for the most part, empty.

Within this perspective, phenomena such as gravity, mass, inertia, and centrifugal effects may be reconsidered not as isolated properties, but as emergent consequences of deeper underlying processes.

This work does not aim to replace established scientific models. Its purpose is to explore whether a complementary conceptual framework can help interpret aspects of reality that remain only partially understood.
